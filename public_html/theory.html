    • The fear of inefficiency and the potential of the military creating dangerous developments with the research money was minimized with the money pooling and the involvement/oversight of civilians through the Congress.
    • Eisenhower established the Advanced Research Projects Agency “ARPA” in charge of the space program, which included Computer Science. It was a centralized agency that does all the research. Brokers would be overseeing the work done by private companies, universities and the military.
    • The way ARPA worked was a request was made by the military (i.e. a more efficient method than switch boarding for phone calls), ARPA recruits private citizens, companies and universities to research more effective methods as replacement, and these “recruits” would be compensated by ARPA.
    • The ARPA system also established the grant money system in universities.
    • ARPA became DARPA: Defense Advanced Research Agency in 1958.
